Talking Clouds

June 2026

Am I late again? Sunday morning - I want to have bubble tea today. The normal ones. The normal ones. A normal life. I want a normal life. You hear people say this in movies. Mostly superheroes. As if I am one - but why? Why a normal life? What is a normal life?

The struggle with getting your weekly wash, the constant toilet cleaning, the making the bed, the commuting, the scrolling, the working, the commuting back, the cooking, the watching a TV show, the eating, the collecting your thoughts - wait a minute, you are already doing all of it! You are living the normal life - you made it. You are doing it. Well done. You have now reached the dreams of all of the superheroes you've seen in the movies. Well done. Nice job. A normal life.

But no - you want more, don't you? A dog. A car. A mortgage. A wife. A baby. A family. You want all of this and more, don't you? More responsibility. More work. Less energy. A normal life plus. Where do I sign up for this subscription?

Do you actually? I don't even know why any of it exists. Can I speak to the person who made this please. Can I speak to your manager please? I am confused about it.

--

Manager: Hi, how I I help? What are you confused about?

Me: All of it - what is it all about?

Manager: What do you mean? Are you referring to the dog?

Me: Yes, sure, why do you get a dog?

Manager: Well, life is better with a dog - have you tried?

Me: Really? No, I haven't - but there's so many of them - how do you even choose one?

Manager: Ah, what type of dog do you like?

Me: Oh I don't know - the one that like me?

Manager: But, do you like them?

Me: I suppose.

Manager: Well, listen, maybe you got to let them choose you too - go have a look, maybe one of them will come to you and say hello.

Me: (I look away, I look up to the sky - I see a long stream of faded clouds, and I look at them - them looking at me. Why are they looking at me?)

Manager: Because you're still here. Because you're still asking. Because you haven't walked away yet. Because you want something to look back.

Me: But they're just clouds. They don't know anything. They don't have eyes. They don't have hands. They don't have a dog or a car or a mortgage or a wife or a baby or a family. They just drift. They just dissolve. They just disappear and no one says goodbye.

Manager: You sound jealous.

Me: I sound tired. I sound like someone who's been standing in a field too long, waiting for the sky to tell me something I don't already know.

Manager: And what do you already know?

Me: That I want things I don't need. That I need things I can't name. That I'm afraid of being ordinary and terrified of being special. That I look at clouds like they're answers when they're just clouds.

Manager: So what do you do?

Me: I don't know. I keep standing here. I keep looking up. I keep waiting. Maybe that's the point. Maybe the clouds aren't looking at me - maybe I'm the one looking at them, and I've forgotten how to look at anything else.

Manager: (The clouds shift, a bird cuts through them, small and green and certain.)

Me: A bird. Not a dog. Not a car. Not a family. Just a bird, going somewhere. It doesn't ask if it's doing it right. It doesn't ask if it's too late. It doesn't ask if it should be something else.

Manager: And what do you learn from the bird?

Me: That you don't learn from the bird. You watch it. You watch it leave. And then you're still here, standing in the field, and the clouds are still there, and the sky is still there, and you're still asking.

Manager: So ask.

Me: Why do I want a normal life when I can't even stand still long enough to live it?

Manager: Because you're human. Because you're alive. Because wanting is what you do. Not having. Wanting.

Me: (A long pause. The bird is gone. The clouds are still. The sky is huge and indifferent and beautiful.)

Me: Okay.

Manager: Okay?

Me: I don't want to be the clouds. I don't want to be the bird. I don't want to be the answer. I want to be the person standing in the field, who doesn't know, who keeps not knowing, and who is learning to be okay with that.

Me: Maybe that's enough.

Manager: Is it?

Me: (I don't answer. I just watch. The clouds keep drifting. The sky keeps being the sky. And I'm still here.)

Me: Yes.
